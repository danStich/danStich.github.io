Teaching

I teach graduate and undergraduate classes in the Biology Department at SUNY Oneonta.

In addition to introductory courses in Ecology and Evolution (BIOL 181) and Ecology (BIOL 282), I teach undergraduate classes in Quantitative Biology and Ichthyology on a rotating basis during spring semesters.

At the graduate level, I teach classes in Advanced Quantitative Biology , Advanced Ichthyology , Lake Management, and special topics in other subjects as needed.

I also teach R workshops like this one for the North American Lake Management Society ( NALMS ), and I have been known to haunt the halls of my alma mater to teach introductory fisheries statistics in FWLD 421 at SUNY Cobleskill.
